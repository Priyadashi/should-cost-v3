# Consolidation Strategies & Quick-Win Playbook

## 8 Consolidation Strategies

### 1. Category Consolidation
**What:** Reduce supplier count within a spend category by directing volume to fewer, preferred suppliers.
**When:** Multiple suppliers provide similar goods/services in the same category.
**Typical savings:** 8-15%
**Implementation:** 2-4 months
**Example:** 5 office supply vendors consolidated to 1 preferred + 1 backup.

### 2. Volume Bundling
**What:** Aggregate fragmented purchases across departments/locations to negotiate volume discounts.
**When:** Same supplier used by multiple BUs at different prices, or same items purchased from different vendors.
**Typical savings:** 10-20%
**Implementation:** 1-3 months
**Example:** 12 locations each buying janitorial supplies independently → single national contract.

### 3. Supplier Elimination
**What:** Remove suppliers that provide negligible value, have poor performance, or duplicate others.
**When:** CPS > 4.0, low spend, low usage frequency, readily available alternatives.
**Typical savings:** 5-10% (admin cost reduction + better pricing from consolidated volume)
**Implementation:** 1-2 months
**Example:** Removing 50 suppliers each with <$5K annual spend, redirecting to catalog suppliers.

### 4. Contract Renegotiation
**What:** Leverage spend data and market benchmarks to renegotiate terms with existing suppliers.
**When:** Expired contracts, above-market pricing, changed volume levels.
**Typical savings:** 5-12%
**Implementation:** 1-3 months
**Example:** Renegotiating IT maintenance contract from T&M to fixed-fee after discovering 30% above market.

### 5. Demand Management
**What:** Reduce unnecessary consumption through policy controls, approval workflows, and specification standardization.
**When:** High transaction volumes, inconsistent specifications, maverick spending.
**Typical savings:** 10-25%
**Implementation:** 2-6 months
**Example:** Standardizing laptop specs from 15 models to 3, reducing unit cost by 18%.

### 6. P-Card / Catalog Migration
**What:** Move low-value, high-frequency transactions to purchasing cards or e-catalogs for process efficiency.
**When:** High volume of small POs (<$2,500), manual requisition processes.
**Typical savings:** 3-8% (process cost savings) + better compliance
**Implementation:** 2-4 months
**Example:** 2,000 annual POs under $1,000 moved to P-Card program, saving $150/PO in process cost.

### 7. Cooperative / GPO Purchasing
**What:** Join a group purchasing organization or cooperative for categories where individual leverage is insufficient.
**When:** Small to mid-size spend in commodity categories, limited individual negotiating power.
**Typical savings:** 5-15%
**Implementation:** 1-2 months
**Example:** Joining a GPO for medical supplies, accessing pre-negotiated pricing 12% below individual rates.

### 8. Spot-Buy Elimination
**What:** Identify and redirect spot purchases (off-contract buying) to contracted suppliers.
**When:** Significant off-contract spend, maverick buying behaviors.
**Typical savings:** 8-20%
**Implementation:** 1-3 months
**Example:** 35% of MRO spend found off-contract; redirecting to preferred supplier saves 15%.

## Quick-Win Identification Criteria

A "quick win" meets ALL of the following:
1. **Savings > $10K** (or user-defined threshold)
2. **Implementation < 90 days**
3. **Low risk** (RS < 0.3)
4. **No contract barrier** (month-to-month or expired)
5. **Alternative exists** (at least 2 qualified alternatives)

### Quick-Win Priority Ranking

| Priority | Criteria | Typical Action |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | Duplicate suppliers in same category, no contract | Eliminate lower performer |
| 2 | Off-contract spend with contracted alternative | Redirect to contract |
| 3 | Expired contract, above-market pricing | Renegotiate or switch |
| 4 | Volume bundling opportunity across locations | Aggregate and renegotiate |
| 5 | P-Card eligible transactions processed as POs | Migrate to P-Card |

## Strategy Selection by Cluster Profile

| Cluster Profile | Primary Strategy | Secondary Strategy |
|----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Many small suppliers, same category | Category Consolidation | Supplier Elimination |
| Few suppliers, large fragmented spend | Volume Bundling | Contract Renegotiation |
| High-frequency, low-value transactions | P-Card Migration | Demand Management |
| Off-contract / maverick spend | Spot-Buy Elimination | Contract Renegotiation |
| Commodity categories, low leverage | GPO / Cooperative | Volume Bundling |
| Inconsistent specifications | Demand Management | Category Consolidation |

## Savings Estimation Formula

```
Estimated Savings = Addressable Spend × Savings Rate × Feasibility Factor

Where:
- Addressable Spend = Total tail spend in cluster eligible for action
- Savings Rate = Category benchmark (see benchmarks.md)
- Feasibility Factor = 0.3 (conservative) to 0.8 (aggressive)
  - 0.3: First-time optimization, no procurement team
  - 0.5: Some procurement maturity, moderate data quality
  - 0.7: Strong procurement team, good data, management support
  - 0.8: Best-in-class procurement, executive sponsorship, clean data
```
